Magpupungko Rock Pools — Complete Guide

Overview

Magpupungko Rock Pools in Pilar, Siargao are one of the island’s most famous natural attractions. These rock pools appear only during low tide when seawater becomes trapped inside naturally carved limestone formations, revealing clear turquoise basins perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and exploring. When the tide rises, the pools disappear beneath stronger surf, making timing incredibly important for anyone planning to visit.

What visitors love most about Magpupungko is the untouched, raw feel of the place. There are no major commercial developments—just waves crashing on the reef, the calm pools protected by rocks, and a wide open beach area where travelers can relax. The surrounding cliffs and rock walls also create dramatic scenery, making the area a favorite for photography and adventurous cliff-jumpers during safe tide conditions.

How to Get There

Most travelers stay in General Luna, and from there, Magpupungko is about a 45-minute drive by scooter. The road is mostly paved and passes through coconut plantations, small villages, and beautiful coastal stretches. Renting a scooter is the most affordable and flexible option, but visitors who don’t drive can hire a habal-habal (motorbike taxi), book a van, or join a land tour that often includes other popular spots like Tayangban Cave Pool.

The entrance area has small snack stalls and shaded huts, but the pools themselves require walking across rocky, uneven terrain—so be prepared.

Costs and Fees

Visiting Magpupungko remains very budget-friendly:

  • Entrance fee: ₱60–₱100 per person
  • Parking fee (motorbike): ₱20–₱30
  • Scooter rental in General Luna: ₱350–₱500 per day
  • Habal-habal round trip: ₱500–₱800 depending on negotiation

There are small eateries near the entrance serving snacks, rice meals, and drinks. Make sure to bring cash, as digital payments are rarely accepted in this part of the island.

Best Time to Visit

The most important factor when visiting Magpupungko is the tide. The pools are only visible and safe for swimming during low tide. At high tide, the pools completely vanish beneath strong waves and currents. For the best experience, arrive 1–2 hours before low tide when the water settles into calm, glassy basins ideal for swimming and photography.

Many bloggers and local guides recommend morning visits—not only for cooler weather but also because crowds tend to build later in the day.

The rocks around the pools can be sharp, so wearing water shoes is highly recommended. Bringing a dry bag for valuables is also smart, as there are no secure lockers on site.

Why Magpupungko is Worth Visiting

Magpupungko Rock Pools offer a stunning mix of adventure and relaxation: clear natural pools, beautiful cliffs, and a beach that’s great for unwinding after exploring the rocks. It’s a must-visit for budget-travelers, backpackers, families, and anyone staying in General Luna looking for a memorable half-day trip.

Whether you want to swim, jump from rocks, take photos, or simply admire the unique scenery, Magpupungko delivers a one-of-a-kind Siargao experience at a very affordable price.
