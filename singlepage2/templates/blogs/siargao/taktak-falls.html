Taktak Falls — Siargao’s Jungle Waterfall Escape

What is Taktak Falls

Taktak Falls is the only easily accessible waterfall on Siargao Island. Nestled in a lush jungle ravine in the municipality of Santa Monica (northern Siargao), the waterfall cascades around 14–15 meters, flowing into a shaded pool surrounded by tropical trees and greenery.

The setting feels peaceful and relaxed — a cool natural pool, forest vibe, and gentle waterfall flow make it a nice contrast from Siargao’s surf and beach spots.

How to Get There

From the main tourist hub of General Luna, expect about a 1.5-hour ride north by motorbike or van to reach Santa Monica / Taktak Falls.

At the right turnoff on the main road, the final stretch is a short drive (or walk) along a dirt path or paved access — signs may say “Taktak Falls 500m turn right.”

Entrance & What It Costs

Visiting is inexpensive. Recent visitor reports list an entrance fee around ₱50 per person, plus a small motorbike parking fee (~₱10).

What to Expect & What to Do

Once you arrive at the falls, you’ll find a small kiosk / hut area at the entrance and a short walk to the pool beneath the falls. The pool is fairly calm and shaded — great for a refreshing dip.

For adventurous visitors, there’s occasionally “tree-jumping” (or small rock jumps) into the pool from overhanging branches or safe ledges — but always check water depth before diving. Some parts of the pool are shallow or have boulders.

The pathway down from the parking/entrance area is relatively short and easy — no long hikes or stairs. This makes Taktak Falls suitable even for travelers who don’t want strenuous trekking.

When to Visit & Practical Tips

The waterfall’s flow depends on rainfall — it tends to be more scenic and refreshing during the wetter season, but can be calmer during dry spells.

Bring water shoes or sandals with grip — rocks near the pool may be slippery or uneven. A dry bag or waterproof pouch helps for phone, wallet, and valuables. Bring cash for entrance and parking.

Because the site is relatively small and simple (not a big resort), don’t expect many amenities — shade, basic seating or huts, maybe small snack stalls.

Why You Might Want to Go

Taktak Falls offers a quiet, nature-filled break from Siargao’s famous surf and beach scenes. It’s refreshing, low-effort, budget-friendly, and perfect for travelers who like a mellow dip and a bit of jungle atmosphere.

It also makes a solid “add-on” to a northern Siargao tour loop — you can combine it with other spots for a full relaxed day trip.
