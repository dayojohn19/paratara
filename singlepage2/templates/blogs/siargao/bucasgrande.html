Bucas Grande can be travelled either from Siargao Island or from Hayanggabon Port in Surigao. Since we came from Butuan (Bancasi airport), we took the latter route.

Here’s an easy, budget-friendly guide for getting Bucas Grande to Jellyfish Sanctuary.

Step 1: From Airport

Outside Bancasi airport, there are readily available passenger vans. Take the van bound to Surigao City and tell the conductor to drop you off at Bad-as junction. Fare is Php 200 (you can haggle down to Php 150 since you will alight before Surigao). Travel time is 2.5 hrs..

Step 2: Ride a Van

Alight at Bad-as junction/market, landmark is Prince Mart. At the junction, there are passenger vans waiting. Ride the van bound to Tandag, tell the conductor to drop you off at Hayanggabon Port. Fare is Php 100 and travel time is 1 hour.

Step 3: Take the Boat

At Hayanggabon Port, charter a boat for the Bucas Grande Island/Sohoton tour. Standard price for a day tour is Php 3,500 per boat..

Step 4: Explore!

At the registration area for Sohoton Cove tour and Jellyfish sanctaury, there is a one time payment of Php 375 per person. Fee is for the paddle boat, tour guides, motor boat, entrance and environmental fee.

Bucas Grande — Sohoton Jellyfish Sanctuary

Bucas Grande Island, located just south of mainland Siargao, is known for its maze of hidden lagoons, limestone cliffs, and its unique Jellyfish Sanctuary. The sanctuary is tucked inside a calm emerald lagoon, only accessible during certain tide conditions. Visitors enter by small paddle or motorized boats through a narrow passageway that feels almost secret — quiet, sheltered, and surrounded by jungle-covered rock walls. The experience is peaceful and intimate, as boat engines are often turned off to maintain the stillness of the water.

The highlight of visiting is the chance to encounter non-stinging jellyfish, which gently float around the sheltered lagoon. These jellyfish have adapted to the environment over time, losing their sting due to the absence of predators. Swimming is allowed but regulated, and touching them is discouraged to protect both the jellyfish and the ecosystem. The feeling of drifting among them is slow, surreal, and calming — almost like moving through a living underwater constellation.

Bucas Grande is usually visited as part of a Sohoton Cove tour, which may also include caves, cliff dives, and additional lagoons. Compared to the busier areas of Siargao, this region remains less commercialized and maintains a sense of quiet nature-first tourism. Expect modest local boats, simple floating docks, and eco-regulated timing for entry to preserve the marine sanctuary.

  • Best Time: Morning to early afternoon, depending on tides.
  • Bring: Dry bag, reef-safe sunscreen, drinking water.
  • Respect: No touching or taking jellyfish from the water.

*Tour prices vary by group size; cash only; weather and tide may affect access.
