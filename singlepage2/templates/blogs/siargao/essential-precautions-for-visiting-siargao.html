Siargao is a stunning island known for surf, lagoons, and laid-back vibes. To fully enjoy your trip, a few smart precautions can help keep your vacation safe and stress-free.

  1. Plan Ahead and Check Weather
    Siargao is prone to heavy rains and storms, especially during typhoon season (July–November). Check weather forecasts, book flexible flights, and allow buffer days in your itinerary.
  2. Protect Your Health
    Bring basic medicines, reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent, and a small first-aid kit. Drink bottled or filtered water and stay hydrated, especially after surfing or island hopping.
  3. Practice Water and Adventure Safety
    Strong currents and sharp corals are common. Wear reef shoes, follow local guides, and avoid swimming alone. Beginners should hire certified surf instructors.
  4. Secure Valuables and Cash
    ATMs are limited and may run out of cash. Bring enough money and keep valuables in waterproof pouches. Use hotel safes when available.
  5. Respect Local Culture and Environment
    Dress modestly in villages, follow local rules, and avoid littering. Support eco-friendly tours and respect marine life during activities.

Approximate Budget (Per Day)

  • Local transport (motorbike/tricycle): ₱300–₱500
  • Food and drinks: ₱500–₱800
  • Island hopping or tours: ₱1,500–₱2,000
  • 1-night budget accommodation: ₱1,000–₱1,800

By staying informed and prepared, you can focus on what matters most—enjoying Siargao’s natural beauty and relaxed island life.
