Cloud 9, Siargao

Your quick guide to Siargao’s world-famous surf spot 🌊

How to Get There

Step 1: Travel to General Luna

From Sayak Airport (Siargao), take a van transfer to General Luna — the island’s main tourist area — for about 45 minutes. Expect to pay around ₱300–₱400 per person for the van ride.

Step 2: Ride to Cloud 9

Rent a motorbike (₱350–₱500 per day) or hire a tricycle (₱50–₱150 per way) to reach Cloud 9 from the town proper. The spot is well-marked and easy to find.

Step 3: Explore the Boardwalk

Cloud 9’s boardwalk extends into the sea, giving you a panoramic view of surfers riding the waves. It’s one of the most photographed spots on the island — especially during sunset.

Step 4: Surf or Watch

The main break is best suited for advanced surfers because of its shallow reef. Beginners can head to nearby **Jacking Horse** or **Quicksilver** for gentler waves. Surfboard rentals cost around ₱200–₱300 per hour, and lessons with an instructor range from ₱500–₱1,000 per hour, including board hire.

Step 5: Best Time to Visit

The peak surfing season runs from **September to December**, when waves are biggest and the atmosphere most vibrant. For calmer crowds and cheaper rates, visit from **January to March** when the weather is still pleasant but less busy.

💸 Fees & Costs Summary

  • Van from airport to General Luna: ₱300–₱400
  • Motorbike rental: ₱350–₱500/day
  • Tricycle fare: ₱50–₱150 one way
  • Surfboard rental: ₱200–₱300/hour
  • Surfing lesson (with instructor): ₱500–₱1,000/hour
  • Entrance fee: None (Free access to boardwalk area)

Plan a minimum budget of around ₱800–₱1,200 for a full half-day visit including transport and surfing if you’re doing lessons, or ₱400–₱600 if just sightseeing.
