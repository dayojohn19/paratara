Siargao Travel Guide 2025 — Surf, Sugba Lagoon & Island Hopping

The essential guide for first-time visitors: where to go, what to do, and how to keep the islands pristine.

Siargao has evolved from a surf-only hideaway into a full island‑hopper’s paradise — think world-class waves at Cloud 9, emerald waters of Sugba Lagoon, and postcard beaches. This guide gives a quick, actionable plan for international travelers visiting in 2025.

Top reasons to visit

World-class Surfing

Cloud 9 is a must for surf enthusiasts and spectators alike.

Sugba Lagoon

Lagoons, paddleboarding, and that viral swing — arrive early for photos.

Island Hopping

Guyam, Daku and Naked Island make for perfect day trips.

Surfing Spots Nearby

  • Cloud 9 – Famous reef break, best for intermediate to advanced surfers.
  • Pacifico – Less crowded, beginner-friendly waves.
  • General Luna Beach – Multiple breaks for all levels.
  • Jacking Horse – Calm beach break for learning.

Party Places & Nightlife

  • RumBar – Popular nightlife spot, live music and DJs.
  • Shaka Bar – Beachfront cocktails, casual party vibe.
  • Bravo Night Events – Hostel-hosted parties on certain nights.

Best Time to Go

Peak surf season: September to November (big waves, great for surfing).

Best overall weather: March to May (sunny, calmer seas, great for beach hopping and island tours).

Note: Typhoon season is June to October – check local weather reports before booking.

Plan your trip wisely, choose hostels according to your budget and party preferences, and enjoy Siargao’s surf culture responsibly!

Quick travel facts

  • Airport: Sayak Airport (IAO) — direct flights from Manila and Cebu.
  • Best time: March–October for surfing; Nov–Apr for calmer seas and sunshine.
  • Currency: Philippine Peso (PHP). Cash is useful on the island.
  • Connectivity: Mobile data available but can be slow during peak season.

Suggested 3-day itinerary (international traveler)

Day 1 — Arrival & Cloud 9

  1. Fly into Sayak (IAO), check into your guesthouse.
  2. Explore General Luna, relax on Cloud 9 boardwalk at sunset.
  3. Grab dinner at a beachfront restaurant — try fresh seafood or inihaw.

Day 2 — Sugba Lagoon & Surfing

  1. Day trip to Sugba Lagoon (book a tour that supports local guides).
  2. Afternoon surf lesson or watch the local surfers at Cloud 9.
  3. Evening: enjoy live acoustic music in General Luna.

Day 3 — Island Hopping & Departure

  1. Island hop: Guyam, Daku, Naked Island — pack sunscreen and picnic snacks.
  2. Return to Sayak for departure; leave extra time for traffic or boat delays.

Where to stay

General Luna is the main hub (cafés, bars, surf schools). For quieter stays, look north toward Pacifico or eco-lodges near Burgos. Consider booking places with strong sustainability practices (water-saving, waste management).

Budget: Beach guesthouse

Simple rooms, great for backpackers and surfers.

Mid-range: Boutique resorts

More comfort, often with breakfast and on-site surf lessons.

Luxury: Eco-resorts

Premium stays with sustainability focus and exclusive beach access.

💰 Complete Budget Guide for Siargao 2025

Daily Budget Estimates

  • Budget Traveler: ₱1,000–₱1,800/day ($20–$35 USD)
  • Mid-Range Traveler: ₱2,000–₱4,000/day ($40–$80 USD)
  • Luxury Traveler: ₱5,000+/day ($100+ USD)

Flights & Transportation

  • Manila to Siargao (round trip): ₱4,000–₱8,000 ($80–$160)
  • Cebu to Siargao (round trip): ₱3,000–₱6,000 ($60–$120)
  • Airport to General Luna (van): ₱300–₱400 ($6–$8)
  • Motorbike rental: ₱350–₱500/day ($7–$10)
  • Bicycle rental: ₱200–₱300/day ($4–$6)
  • Tricycle rides: ₱50–₱150 ($1–$3)

Accommodation (Per Night)

  • Budget hostel dorm: ₱400–₱800 ($8–$16)
  • Budget private room: ₱1,000–₱1,800 ($20–$35)
  • Mid-range hotel/resort: ₱2,500–₱4,500 ($50–$90)
  • Luxury resort: ₱6,000–₱15,000+ ($120–$300+)

Activities & Tours

Money-Saving Tips 2025

  • Book flights 2-3 months in advance for best prices
  • Travel during shoulder season (Jan-March) for lower rates
  • Split boat/tour costs with other travelers
  • Eat at local carinderias instead of tourist restaurants
  • Rent scooters weekly for discounts (₱2,000–₱2,500/week)
  • Join hostel-organized group tours to split costs
  • Bring refillable water bottle (many refill stations available)
  • Download offline maps before arriving

What to Bring Cash For

  • Most tours and boat rentals are cash-only
  • Small restaurants and street vendors
  • Scooter rentals and deposits
  • ATMs can run out during peak season—bring enough pesos from Manila/Cebu
