Sugba Lagoon, Siargao

A step-by-step travel guide

How to Get There

Sugba Lagoon is one of Siargao’s most beautiful natural attractions — a turquoise lagoon surrounded by limestone cliffs. Here’s how to get there easily and what to expect.

Step 1: Go to Del Carmen Port

Head to the main boat terminal in Del Carmen — you can reach it by motorbike or car from General Luna (around 45 minutes).

Step 2: Park & Register

There’s parking space at the port. Once you arrive, go inside and find the registration booth for Sugba Lagoon tours.

Step 3: Book a Boat

Choose a boat to Sugba Lagoon. You can rent privately or share one with up to 6 people. You’ll also pay an environmental fee.

Step 4: Boat Ride (25 minutes)

Enjoy a scenic ride through the mangroves — a peaceful trip before you reach the lagoon’s clear waters.

Step 5: Arrive at the Floating Pontoon House

This is the main hub where you can rent paddle boards, kayaks, and jump from the diving board into the lagoon!

Step 6: Enjoy and Explore

Relax, swim, and take in the view. There’s a 3-hour limit per group, and no food or alcohol is allowed inside the lagoon.

Travel Tips

  • Go early to avoid crowds at the diving board.
  • Split boat fees with other travelers to save money.
  • Bring water, snacks, and eco-friendly sunscreen.
  • Don’t forget your waterproof camera!

Sugba Lagoon — Overview

Sugba Lagoon is one of the most iconic natural gems of Siargao, located in the quiet municipality of Del Carmen. Nestled among dense mangrove forests and limestone formations, the lagoon is known for its clear emerald water that shifts in shades depending on the weather and sunlight. The surrounding environment is calm and almost untouched, making it a refreshing contrast from the surfing crowds found around General Luna. The lagoon is accessible by a scenic boat ride that passes through a protected mangrove sanctuary, where the silence of the water and the slow movement of the boat set the tone for a peaceful escape.

The floating wooden boathouse in the center of the lagoon serves as a base point for most visitors. From here, you can rent stand-up paddleboards, kayaks, snorkeling masks, and life vests. Many travelers enjoy simply paddling into quieter corners of the lagoon, where the water becomes shallow and glass-smooth, perfect for swimming or photographing the reflections of the cliffs and sky. There is also a small diving platform where you can jump into the deeper part of the lagoon. The atmosphere is generally slow-paced and relaxed, suited for travelers who prefer nature experiences over busy beach club scenes.

  • Travel Tip: Go early morning for fewer crowds and calmer water.
  • Best For: Nature lovers, couples, peaceful adventure seekers.
  • Bring: Dry bag, eco-safe sunscreen, water, and light snacks.

*Entrance fees, equipment rentals, and boat rates vary by season and group size. Cash only.
