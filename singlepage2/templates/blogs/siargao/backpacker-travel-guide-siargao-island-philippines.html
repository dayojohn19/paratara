Backpacker Travel Guide: Siargao Island, Philippines

Location: Siargao Island, Philippines

Overview

Siargao Island is a top backpacker destination in Southeast Asia, originally famous for surfing but now attracting digital nomads, expats, and adventure travelers. With stunning beaches, surf breaks, lush landscapes, hidden lagoons, and affordable prices, Siargao offers a balance of island paradise and convenience. The main tourist hub is General Luna, but the island remains largely authentic and uncrowded.

Things To Know Before Going

  • Siargao is teardrop-shaped and has one airport (IAO).
  • Reliable Wi-Fi and electricity in major towns; spottier in rural areas.
  • Best travel insurance options include SafetyWing plans starting at $2/day.

How To Get There

Flights from Cebu or Manila connect to Siargao. Shared vans from the airport to your accommodation cost around 300 PHP. Transfers from other towns in the Philippines require connecting flights.

Where To Stay

  • Mad Monkey Siargao: Party-focused hostel for solo travelers.
  • Casavia: Boutique hostel ideal for digital nomads.
  • Hiraya Surf Hostel & Harana Surf Resort: Great mid-range options near the beach.

Getting Around

Motorbikes are the most convenient way to explore the island. Taxis and vans are available but less flexible.

Best Things To Do

  • Surf at Cloud 9, the world-famous break.
  • Visit Sugba Lagoon and other hidden lagoons.
  • Island hopping to nearby spots like Naked Island and Guyam Island.
  • Explore local markets, cafes, and street food in General Luna.

Nightlife

General Luna offers a vibrant nightlife scene with bars, beach parties, and live music. Mad Monkey Hostel is popular for social events.

Tips for Travelers

  • Bring cash as ATMs can be limited outside major towns.
  • Respect local customs and natural areas.
  • Travel insurance is recommended for scooters and adventure sports.

💰 Complete Budget Guide for Backpackers

Daily Budget Estimates

  • Budget Backpacker: ₱800–₱1,500 per day (₱16–₱30 USD)
  • Mid-Range Traveler: ₱1,500–₱3,000 per day (₱30–₱60 USD)
  • Comfortable Traveler: ₱3,000–₱5,000+ per day (₱60–₱100+ USD)

Transportation Costs

  • Flight from Manila to Siargao: ₱2,000–₱5,000 (book early for best prices)
  • Flight from Cebu to Siargao: ₱1,500–₱3,500
  • Airport to General Luna (shared van): ₱300–₱400
  • Motorbike rental: ₱350–₱500 per day
  • Fuel per day: ₱100–₱200
  • Tricycle rides (local): ₱50–₱150
  • Habal-habal (motorbike taxi): ₱100–₱300 depending on distance

Accommodation Costs

  • Hostel dorm bed: ₱400–₱800 per night
  • Budget private room: ₱800–₱1,500 per night
  • Mid-range hotel/resort: ₱1,500–₱3,500 per night
  • Upscale resort: ₱3,500–₱8,000+ per night

Food & Drinks

  • Local carinderia meal: ₱60–₱120
  • Street food snacks: ₱20–₱80
  • Restaurant meal (tourist area): ₱200–₱400
  • Coffee at cafe: ₱80–₱150
  • Bottled water: ₱20–₱40
  • Fresh fruit shake: ₱60–₱120
  • Beer at bar: ₱80–₱150
  • Cocktails: ₱150–₱300

Activities & Tours

  • Cloud 9 surf lesson (1 hour): ₱500–₱1,000
  • Surfboard rental: ₱200–₱300 per hour
  • Island hopping tour (3 islands): ₱1,500–₱2,500 per person
  • Sugba Lagoon tour: ₱1,200–₱2,000 per person (or split boat costs)
  • Magpupungko Rock Pools entrance: ₱60–₱100
  • Sohoton Cove/Bucas Grande day tour: ₱2,000–₱3,500 per person
  • Paddleboard rental: ₱200–₱300 per hour
  • Snorkeling gear rental: ₱100–₱200

Sample 7-Day Budget Breakdown

Budget Backpacker (7 days):

  • Accommodation (hostel): ₱2,800 (7 nights × ₱400)
  • Food (local meals): ₱2,800 (₱400/day)
  • Motorbike rental: ₱2,500 (7 days)
  • Fuel: ₱1,000
  • Activities (2-3 tours): ₱3,000
  • Total: ₱12,100 (~$240 USD) for 7 days

Mid-Range Traveler (7 days):

  • Accommodation (private room): ₱8,400 (7 nights × ₱1,200)
  • Food (mix of local & restaurants): ₱4,900 (₱700/day)
  • Motorbike rental: ₱3,000
  • Fuel: ₱1,400
  • Activities (4-5 tours): ₱8,000
  • Total: ₱25,700 (~$510 USD) for 7 days

Money-Saving Tips

  • Split boat tours and rentals with other travelers
  • Eat at local carinderias instead of tourist restaurants
  • Book flights 2-3 months in advance
  • Stay in hostels with kitchens to cook your own meals
  • Rent motorbikes for longer periods (weekly rates are cheaper)
  • Visit during off-peak season (Jan-March) for lower prices
  • Bring a refillable water bottle (many places have water refill stations)

Conclusion

Siargao Island offers a mix of adventure, relaxation, and culture, making it a must-visit for backpackers and digital nomads alike. Plan ahead for transportation, accommodation, and safety, and you'll enjoy an unforgettable island experience.

Best Things To Do in Siargao Island, Philippines

Location: Siargao Island, Philippines

Introduction

Siargao Island is known as the surfing capital of the Philippines, but it offers so much more for travelers. From pristine beaches and hidden lagoons to island hopping and local culinary experiences, here’s your ultimate guide to the best things to do on this beautiful island.

1. Surfing at Cloud 9

The world-famous Cloud 9 surf break is a must for surfers. Beginners can take lessons, while experienced surfers can ride the powerful waves. The iconic Cloud 9 boardwalk is perfect for watching competitions and stunning sunsets.

2. Explore Sugba Lagoon

Sugba Lagoon is a turquoise paradise perfect for paddleboarding, kayaking, swimming, and cliff jumping. Take a guided tour to fully enjoy the lagoon and its surrounding mangroves.

3. Island Hopping

  • Naked Island: Small sandbar island for sunbathing and swimming.
  • Guyam Island: Quiet, picturesque beach with coconut palms.
  • Daku Island: Local seafood and beach picnic experience.

4. Visit Magpupungko Rock Pools

Natural tidal pools that appear during low tide, ideal for swimming and cliff jumping. Be sure to check the tide schedule for the best experience.

5. Explore Local Towns & Markets

General Luna is the main hub with cafes, bars, and local markets. Try local Filipino street food, buy souvenirs, and experience the island’s laid-back vibe.

6. Coconut Farm & Palm Grove Walks

Siargao is full of lush coconut plantations. Walking or cycling through the palm groves is a peaceful way to see the rural side of the island.

7. Nightlife & Beach Parties

Experience the island’s social side with beach parties, live music, and bars. Popular spots include Mad Monkey Hostel and local bars in General Luna.

Tips for Enjoying Siargao

  • Rent a motorbike to explore the island independently.
  • Carry cash as ATMs are limited outside main towns.
  • Respect nature and local culture during excursions.
  • Bring sun protection, insect repellent, and reusable water bottles.

Conclusion

Siargao Island offers adventure, relaxation, and authentic island culture. Whether you’re surfing, swimming in lagoons, or island hopping, these activities will make your trip unforgettable.
