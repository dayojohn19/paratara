Coconut Trees View Deck — Siargao’s Sea of Palms

Why Visit

Coconut Trees View Deck offers one of the most iconic vistas on Siargao — a seemingly endless canopy of coconut palms stretching toward distant hills and horizon. From its elevated roadside vantage point, you see a lush, green landscape that captures the island’s tropical beauty, making it a must-stop for travelers exploring beyond the beaches and surf spots.

How to Get There

The viewpoint lies along the main island road, the Siargao Circumferential Road. From General Luna, expect roughly 25–30 minutes by motorbike. The road is paved and easy for riders. Because it lies along the main route, visiting the deck makes for a convenient stop when heading to other destinations like Maasin River, northern beaches, or northern land tours.

Access & Fees

There is no entrance fee or formal parking — the “deck” is essentially a roadside pull-off/overlook. You simply slow down or park on the shoulder and enjoy the view. For many, it’s just a quick stop for photos or a short break from a longer ride around the island.

💰 Budget & Costs

Visiting Coconut Trees View Deck is essentially free. Here's a cost breakdown for your trip:

  • Entrance Fee: FREE
  • Parking: FREE (roadside pull-off)
  • Motorbike Rental (from General Luna): ₱350–₱500 per day
  • Fuel (round trip from General Luna): ₱100–₱150
  • Fresh Coconut (from local vendors): ₱50–₱80
  • Snacks (if available): ₱50–₱100

Total Estimated Budget: ₱500–₱800 for the day trip including motorbike rental and fuel. This makes it one of the most budget-friendly attractions on Siargao, perfect for travelers wanting scenic photo opportunities without spending much.

What to Expect

At the viewpoint, you’ll often be greeted with calm, green scenery: thousands of palm trees forming a dense canopy that seems to flow like waves of green. The view is especially stunning during golden hour — either at sunrise or just before sunset — when light softens and the sea of palms glows beautifully.

Some locals may offer fresh coconut water or light snacks nearby. There are no formal facilities or restrooms — so plan accordingly and bring water, sunscreen, and be mindful of traffic nearby while parking or walking around.

How It Fits Your Siargao Trip

Because the viewpoint lies along the main loop road, it's easy to include it as a “bonus stop” in any land-tour or island hop. Whether you’re heading north, exploring inland, or cruising between beaches, a quick detour to the Coconut Trees View Deck gives you a glimpse of Siargao’s natural heart — its signature coconut-filled landscape.

It’s ideal for a short visit: a few photos, a bit of calm, and then back on the road — perfect for travellers with limited time or those combining multiple spots in one day.
