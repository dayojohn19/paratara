Living or doing business in Siargao means adapting to island life. One simple upgrade that makes a big difference is having a freezer fridge at home or in your rental.

  1. Choose the right size. Small households do well with a 6–8 cu.ft. unit, while homestays and cafés benefit from larger, energy-efficient models.
  2. Plan for power stability. Look for inverter models that handle voltage changes and consume less electricity.
  3. Arrange local delivery. Many appliance sellers deliver from Surigao or nearby towns; confirm warranty and service options.
  4. Organize smart storage. Use labeled containers so frozen fish, meat, and ice last longer and are easy to find.
  5. Maintain regularly. Defrost when needed, clean seals, and keep airflow clear to extend lifespan.

Having a freezer fridge in Siargao helps you buy in bulk, reduce market trips, store fresh catch, and prepare for days when supplies run low. It’s especially useful during peak tourist seasons and rough weather.

Approximate Costs (₱)

  • Appliance transport/delivery: ₱2,000–₱5,000
  • Local fare (trike/van while shopping): ₱300–₱800
  • Food while arranging purchase: ₱500–₱1,000
  • 1-night budget stay (if needed): ₱1,500–₱3,000

With proper planning, a freezer fridge quickly pays off by saving time, money, and stress—making island living more comfortable.
