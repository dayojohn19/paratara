How to get in Tourist Destination

Location: Laiya Batangas

San Juan, Batangas — Quick Summary

San Juan (Laiya) is a coastal town with powdery beaches, island coves and reef-protected snorkeling areas — ideal for island-hopping, watersports, resort staycations and light adventure activities. Many visitors base themselves in Laiya to access beach-hopping and water activities.

How to get there (simple steps)

  1. From Manila drive south on the STAR/MANILA–BATANGAS route — aim for San Juan (Laiya) area as your destination.
  2. If commuting: take public transport to San Juan then a jeep/tricycle to Laiya (approx. ₱50 fare, ~30 min from San Juan center).
  3. At Laiya head to popular jump-off points (e.g., Sigayan Bay Resort or Kabayan Resort) to book island-hopping or water activities.
  4. If you rent a private car, you can drive directly to the resorts and beaches; parking is usually available at resort properties.

Top places & things to do

  • Lamesang Bato (Locoloco Point) — scenic beach/cove accessible by boat.
  • Bato na Gulagod (Alegre) — cliff-backed cove, often included in island-hopping.
  • Island hopping & snorkeling — visit 1–2 coves and snorkeling areas around Laiya.
  • Laiya Adventure Park — zipline, ATV, wall-climbing and packaged activities.
  • Solmera Coast / beach resorts — resort staycations and beachfront facilities (pools, bikes, kayaks).
  • Heritage houses, San Juan Nepomuceno Church & ruins, mangrove tours and birdwatching.
  • Food trip: local eateries, cottage industry souvenirs and small seaside stalls on the islands (bring packed lunch when island-hopping).

Typical costs & rates (from the article)

Island hopping (Laiya)

  • Per person rate (shared): ₱300 (minimum 10 pax per boat) or rent whole boat: ₱3,000 per boat (good for ~10 pax). Docking fee ₱100 per boat. Snorkel mask rental: ₱100. Duration ≈ 3 hours.
  • Optional add-on to include Bato na Gulagod (Alegre): ≈ ₱1,000 per boat.

Sample 2-day itinerary (compact)

  1. Day 1 — Morning: Arrive in San Juan / check-in to resort. Midday island-hopping (3 hours) — Lamesang Bato + snorkeling. Evening: beach dinner.
  2. Day 1 — Night: Relax at resort or explore local eateries.
  3. Day 2 — Morning: Laiya Adventure Park (zipline / ATV) or water sports (jet ski / kayak).
  4. Day 2 — Afternoon: Heritage walk (church / ruins) + mangrove or birdwatching (if time permits). Depart late afternoon.

Travel tips & reminders

  • Start island-hopping early (around 8:00 AM) to avoid crowds and enjoy calmer seas. 1}
  • Bring aqua shoes — reef and coral deposits can be sharp. 1}
  • Bring water, packed lunch and small cash for boat crew, island stalls and docking fees.
  • Confirm activity rates with resorts or operators before booking; prices above are article-reported approximations and may change.
